Strong Family Values

Family is at the heart of Filipino culture. Close-knit family ties extend beyond the nuclear family to include grandparents, cousins, aunts, uncles, and even close friends considered as family. Celebrations and decisions often involve the entire clan

Warm Hospitality

Filipinos are famous for their warm and generous hospitality. Visitors are treated with utmost respect, offered food and drinks, and often invited to join family gatherings. Hospitality is deeply ingrained and practiced in all communities.

Faith and Religion

About 80% of Filipinos are Roman Catholic, with Christianity and Islam being the most practiced religions. Religious festivals, Sunday mass, and daily prayers are common influencing personal and public life across the islands.

Bayanihan Spirit

Bayanihan is a Filipino term that embodies the spirit of communal unity and cooperation. It is often illustrated by neighbors helping each other, especially in times of need, such as moving houses or during disasters.

Work Ethic and Education

Filipinos are known for their strong work ethic, resilience, and dedication to education. Many pursue higher education and professional careers, while also valuing vocational skills. Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) contribute significantly to the economy.
